Smart On-Demand
Flight Booking System

Role Conceptualization
Team Individual Project
Duration 1 Week

The Problem

The challenge was to disrupt the passenger experience in the aviation industry, at any point of the passenger's interaction with the industry.


The Solution

My solution is to introduce smart computational algorithms to automate the flight ticket booking systems to cut down flight booking times by a considerable margin.


The current flight booking system involves a total of seven steps spread across primary sets of details and additional customer details. These steps, some critical and some not, become redundant the more you book flight tickets and can be predicted over regular use of a portal.

The increased number of steps leads to two fundamental problems involving human psychology. These problems are Cognitive Overload and Paradox of Choice, explained below.

One fundamental aspect of human psychology is that humans love patterns. Humans have a tendency to fall into behavioral patterns that can be predicted. This is where Mahcine Learning comes into the picture. Using advanced computing algorithms to predict the outcome of flight booking tendencies, the entire process can be made simpler.

With this, the entire process can be brought down to two simple steps, drastically reducing the booking times from 30 minutes on average to less than 2 minutes.

Data is king. For any prediction to be made, the algorithm requires data to sort through. This data is available with the operators who run current flight booking portals and from passengers who are willing to divulge this information towards the machine learning model.

With the right strides taken towards the right direct, booking flgiht tickets one day could be as simple as saying "Hey Google, book me a ticket to Guwahati on the 12th this month" and it could be done!




Learning

This challenge gave me the opportunity understand the intricate details behind booking flight tickets, a process I myself was intimidated by, up until then! To be able to understand the system and optimise it gave me an opprtunity to benefit not just the millions of passengers who fly everyday but also my family and I directly.


Scope

Given more time to work on this challenge, I would have interacted with more participants to help me refine the scope of the solution and conceptualise a dedicated solution. I would also have designed possible deliverables that would reflect how the sysem would work.
