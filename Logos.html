Thirty Logos
Challenge

Role Brand Identity Design
Team Individual Project
Duration 4 Weeks, Winter 2017

The Idea

The Thirty Logos Challenge was a series of life-like design briefs sent every day for 30 days where I was tasked to design the logos according to the design briefs. There below are my 30 logos according to the briefs.


Day 1

Space is building coworking offices so that freelancers and small startup companies have a stunning office to work out of without paying the big bucks to buy or lease a large building. For the Space logo, we want to capture the idea of a personal, modern, and fun shared office space.


Day 2

The Grind is a small coffee shop chain located in Seattle, WA with five locations. The Grind prides itself on natural and local ingredients. For their new logo, they do not want to use any browns! So many coffee shops around there use brown and they would like to stand out.


Day 3

Twitchy Rabbit is an email marketing platform similar to MailChimp and Twitchy the Rabbit is their mascot. They would like him to be redesigned into a more simple, iconic form that they can use across their products.


Day 4

Ping is a growing chat platform for businesses and small teams. Some of their biggest competitors are Slack and Skype. What sets them apart is that they offer major features like group screen sharing, free of charge.


Day 5

Wildlife is a non-profit organization that preserves the life and habitat of wild animals throughout the world. Their organization is known for their passion and personal investment in the lives of animals.


Day 6

1-800-Rosebud is an online flower shop which sells bouquets of flowers and gifts for any special occasion.


Day 7

Austin Run is a charitable event for autism research and raises money for families in need of care, education, and information. They are a large running event that happens once a year in Austin, Texas and had over 5,000 participants last year. The event is so much fun and many people dress up in crazy outfits.


Day 8

Sparked is a video game news website featuring the latest news in all video games, esports, and tech.


Day 9

Paint is a mobile app that allows you to take a photo of anything and it will automatically pull up the correct color in multiple formats including Sherwin Williams Paint, PANTONE Color, and many more.


Day 10

Anchor is a clothing line for the nautical lovers! Many of their products are produced with recycled boat sail material.


Day 11

Sweets offer a very wide variety of candy; just about anything imaginable.


Day 12

Sword & Shield is an upcoming security system for tech companies. Their software includes password protection, server backups, malware protection, and more.They are focused on appealing to medium to large scale technology companies.


Day 13

JJ Pizza has been serving the Chicago area with the very best pizza since 2002. They are rated #1 for the area.


Day 14

BookWorm is a growing online bookstore.


Day 15

Pups is a new online subscription service focused on dog food, toys, and medicine delivery. Their users sign up for weekly or monthly delivery of premium dog food straight to their door! While a large bulk of their subscribers are for dog food, they also deliver toys, treats, and medicine.


Day 16

Sharp has been creating high quality cooking knives for over a decade. They are known for their craftsmanship, quality, and outstanding cuts.


Day 17

Fast is an online form generator where users can generate and interact with any kind of form like W9, contracts, etc. It's perfect for freelancers and home business.


Day 18

Sakura is a sushi bar in downtown Los Angelas. Sakura means flowering cherry (blossom) tree.


Day 19

Hampton Cove Animal Hospital.'s main focus is pets/veterinary/animal hospital. The Hampton Cove area is known for it's mountains.


Day 20

Freelance is an online system that helps freelancers (designers, artists, and all other creative types) manage their invoices, time, projects, client communication, etc. in one suite.


Day 21

Sprinkles is an ice cream shop with stores across the United States. They pride themselves in the best tasting ice cream on the planet. They have won numerous awards for our taste and innovative flavors.


Day 22

Design your own personal logo. (This has since changed to what it is currently!)


Day 23

Deadbeat is an online music community focused on EDM, Drum & Bass, House, Dubstep, etc. type of music. Their close competitors are Monstercat and 99Lives.


Day 24

Avocado is an upcoming app for smart grocery shopping! Their bundle includes a mount for your phone to attach to the handle of your grocery cart/buggy and will scan each item as you put it in your cart. This keeps track of all kinds of awesome data including total price, calories and other nutritional information, recipe ideas based on what's inthe cart, and more!


Day 25

The Wenatchee National Forest is home to beautiful mountains, water, and hiking trails. They need a logo to represent the forest and the amazing mountain views. This logo will be used by the forest staff including advertising for camping, staff uniforms, website, etc.


Day 26

My Wine is a new online service that delivers premium wine to your doorstep. You choose from a variety of tastes and preferences to pair up with your dinner plans.


Day 27

This company works directly with the National Football League and gets real-time health data from the athletes during games and practices. Each player wears a FitBit and everything from their heart rate to their speed gets sent to their app.


Day 28

Fashionista is a women's fashion app that pairs your current wardrobe with amazing deals at local stores around you. For example, a user could enter in two pairs of jeans and one yellow shirt, and our app will return with other clothes that pair well with the those jeans and shirt as well as complimented outfits.


Day 29

FILM is a non-profit supporting film students with scholarships, free trips, etc. to reach their career goals. They have helped many well-known directors while they were students including some who had blockbuster success.


Day 30

For the final challenge, redesign a logo of your choice. This could be your uncle's burger shop, an app you use that nobody knows about, or even something as known as the Nike logo.

Over the course of the entire month, I could visibly see my grasp over visual design branding evolve in terms of my color sense and aesthetic. Adhering to a strict 30 days was difficult, and I am guilty of skipping a day or two, but it was super satisfying to reach the end of the 30 days and be able to come up with 30 unique logos.
